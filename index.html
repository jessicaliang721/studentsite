Who We Are

Asian Christian Fellowship at Washington University exists to be an environment where broken people discover and respond to the grace of God. ACF seeks to be a community committed to the deepening of faith in Jesus Christ by fostering an environment of open, honest, spiritual dialogue.

Our mission is to reach the Asian community by helping all people encounter God and grow closer to Jesus.

How Can I Participate?

Large Group

Every Friday night, ACF gathers to worship through song, message and prayer. We start the night by singing songs of worship then receive a short message by an invited speaker. Topics may include God's perspectives on school, friendships, and relationships. Afterwards, we enjoy more fellowship together through a social activity (food, movies, games, or laser tag!).

We meet every Friday night at 7pm in Simon Hall, room 122.

Small Group

During small group, students gather to learn about and discuss short Bible passages or other topics related to Christianity. Each small group has leader(s) who will prepare for and lead during that time. We also have devotions and prayer meetings in addition to our small groups. Everyone is welcome to attend!

Join us in these small groups throughout the week to discuss and explore issues we encounter in our lives and what God has to say about them!

LOL (Live Out Loud)

Mondays, 7:00-8:30 pm

Mudd 130

We’ll be talking about what it looks like to live a Christian life, specifically on the college campus. We’ll look at both what the Bible has to say about this subject and discuss some pressing topics, such as partying and relationships.

Running the Race

Wednesdays, 7:30-9:30 pm

Nemerov Seminar A

Running the Race is a small group exploring the interesting combination of Asian/Asian-American and Christian identities. Come for discussion about how both of these cultures influence one another, how they both influence us, and how we can react as Asian-Americans and/or Christians to the broader racial issues in America. Also, Cal will bring cookies.

Wednesday Small Group

Wednesdays, 8:00-9:30 pm

Eliot B1 common room

Our small group aspires to build a close-knit community of people who desire to know both each other and Scripture on a deeper level. This semester, we will be looking into the book of Genesis and going back to our foundation in faith by delving into the meaning behind God and His Creation.

Unapologetically Christian

Thursdays, 7:00-9:00 pm

Gregg 3 study room

We are a small group directed at allowing both Christians and non-Christians to come together to talk about questions pertaining to faith. We encourage people of all backgrounds to come and represent your ideas freely, without fear of judgment.

Nemerov Small Group

Thursdays, 7:00-9:00 pm

Nemerov Seminar A

This small group is a community of people seeking to know more of who Jesus was (and is!) through the gospel of Matthew.

Events

ACF holds many events throughout the year – you are welcome to join us!

Fall

Ice Blocking

Join ACF in a day of fun on the slippery slopes of Art Hill! Every year during the first month of school ACF goes ice blocking! What is ice blocking you may ask? It is exactly like it sounds, we slide down Art Hill on giant blocks of ice. Bring a T-Shirt so your butt doesn't get cold! Trust us, it is a ton of fun.

The Amazing Race

Nothing builds fellowship more than some friendly competition! Every year ACF holds an event called the amazing race. We are split into teams and much complete and intense campus wide scavenger hunt! Winners receive bragging rights and some delicious custard from Mr. Wizards - a St. Louis specialty. The amazing race is a fun way to get to know campus and also a good way to get to know ACFers better!

Apple Picking

When the time is ripe, ACF likes to go out as a fellowship to Eckert's Orchard to have fun picking and eating apples! Ride are provided. Come out to this day of fun and feasting! (Apple baseball is a must).

Winter

Urbana

Every three years, students from all over the US (and abroad) gather in downtown St. Louis for a Missions conference to learn about God's global mission and what God has planned for them.. Students listen to and meet with missionaries from all over the world and to learn how God moves in various places. The conference is hosted by Intervarsity Christian Fellowship and lasts from after Christmas Day through New Years! Learn more at urbana.org.

BroSis

In the middle of second semester, ACF holds BroSis, a week-long event where Brothers and Sisters appreciate each other and show God's love to each other.To show sacrificial love, throughout the week, one group gives gifts to the other group, and at of the week, the other group throws a banquet in appreciation!

Ohana

After returning from Winter Break in January, ACF attends Ohana, a new Intervarsity conference centered on the Asian American perspective on Christianity. Since Ohana is a new conference, we do not know when the next Ohana will be, but we do look forward to attending once again!

Spring

CityLights

During Spring break, students can choose to take an urban plunge and serve the community in St. Louis. In collaboration with City Lights Church, students from around the US gather to learn about how God works in the lives of the poor and broken in the nearby city.

Outpour Church Baptism

Near Easter, Outpour Church holds baptisms that students are invited to participate in or attend. Often many members of ACF choose to celebrate with other students who have chosen to proclaim their faith in Christ.

Catalyst

At the end of the school year, ACF ventures to Camp Windermere in Camdenton, MO for a final retreat of the school year. During Catalyst we grow closer as a community while learning more about Christ-centered leadership. When not in seminars or Bible-study, we enjoy the camp by exploring nature, hiking or playing on inflatable play-structures in the lake. For more information, check out the Catalyst website: http://ivcatalyst.com

F.A.Q

Q: I'm not Asian or Christian. Can I still be part of ACF?
A: Of course - we welcome everyone to ACF!

Q: What else does ACF do on campus?
A: ACF is a fellowship made up of students with diverse interests. Many members of ACF are also active in other campus groups such as Lunar New Year Festival (LNYF), Asian American Association (AAA), and more. Furthermore, ACF participates in Intramural (IM) sports - contact Brandon Eng for more info!

Q: I'm new and I want to be part of ACF - what do I do?
A: Join us for large group or small group! We also have many events throughout the year that you can join. We would love to see you there!

Q: How do I become more involved in ACF?
A: Join us in our events or consider applying for leadership in the spring semester!

Q: I'm not even at Wash U yet, how do I learn more?
A: Check out our Facebook page or contact us!
